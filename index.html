RESEARCH (link)


The first paper I worked on as an RA focuses on intersectional wage discrimination and the concept of excessive privilege. We examined the effects of overlapping identities on wage gaps, particularly the intersectional effects of gender and race in the US labor market.

Key findings from our research:

  • We discovered that White males (WM) experience unique privilege compared to all other groups, which we termed "excessive White male privilege".
  • Contrary to previous literature, we did not find evidence for a unique intersectional wage penalty for Black females (BF) compared to all others.
  • Our findings challenge common methods of measuring intersectional wage discrimination and highlight the importance of recognizing and addressing systemic privilege.

This research provides a novel tool for intersectionality studies and policy aimed at creating a more equitable society. It emphasizes the need for more refined metrics in capturing wage discrimination and the importance of considering excessive privilege in analyses of wage gaps.

🔬 Superconductors

Machine Learning scikit-learn Feature Engineering

Predicting the critical temperature of superconductors can help further our understanding of superconductivity and aid in designing and discovering new materials. It is also useful for the advancement of quantum computing technology, which heavily relies on superconductors.



In this project, I utilized a range of tree-based models to predict the critical temperature of superconductors. Random Forests and XGBoost were the best performing models. For interpretability, the Random Forest model is recommended. The feature importance analysis reveals which properties of the superconductors have the most predictive power in determining their critical temperature.

Key Findings:

  • Random Forest and XGBoost models performed the best in predicting the critical temperature.
  • Feature importance analysis shows that range_ThermalConductivity is the most significant feature.
  • Increasing the number of estimators in ensemble models reduces variance and improves performance.

The dataset was sourced from the UCI machine learning repository.
